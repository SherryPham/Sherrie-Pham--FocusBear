# Professionalism at Focus Bear — Reflection

## Research & Learn

### Professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual environment
Casual doesn't mean careless. Professional looks like: showing up on time, communicating clearly when something will be late, owning mistakes without spiralling. Unprofessional looks like: ghosting on commitments, talking over people, oversharing personal grievances in work channels, public passive-aggression, gossip, or treating "we're chill here" as licence to skip basic respect.

### Respectful communication in remote teams
- Default to a kind, neutral tone in writing — text strips out warmth, so err on the warmer side.
- Assume good intent. If a message reads as rude, ask before reacting.
- Disagree with the idea, not the person. "I think this approach has a problem because…" not "you're wrong."
- Use the right channel for urgency (email for non-urgent, Discord for same-day, SMS/call for genuinely urgent).
- Respond to messages even if just to say "got it, will get back to you tomorrow."

### Giving and receiving feedback professionally
- **Giving:** specific, focused on actions not personality, in private if it's constructive, with a clear example. "In the standup yesterday, X happened — could we try Y next time?"
- **Receiving:** assume the person is trying to help you, not attack you. Ask clarifying questions instead of defending. Take a beat before responding if it stings — "let me think on that and come back" is fine.

## Reflection

### Great teamwork I've experienced
The teams I've worked best with had three things in common: clear ownership (everyone knew what was theirs), short and useful meetings, and a genuine willingness to flag problems early rather than hide them until they were unfixable.

### Making my communication clear, respectful, inclusive
- Read messages back before sending — cut anything that could land as snippy.
- Lead with the point, then the context.
- Avoid sarcasm and idioms in writing; they don't travel well across cultures or neurotypes.
- Use names rather than "guys" or "you lot."
- Acknowledge when I don't know something rather than bluffing.

### Helping create a positive, professional environment
Show up prepared, follow through on commitments, give credit publicly, raise concerns privately, and contribute to small things (responding to teammates' messages, celebrating wins) that make the place feel human.

### Preparing for meetings with supervisors
- Send a calendar invite with a clear title (project name, not just "Focus Bear") and a short agenda.
- Do 10 minutes of homework first — try the obvious search/AI lookup before asking.
- Write down questions in advance so I don't waste time figuring out what I want to ask.
- Take notes during the meeting and capture action items at the end.
- Follow up afterwards confirming what was decided and what I'm doing next.

### Being proactive during the internship
- Send a weekly progress update without being asked.
- Try things and inform, rather than ask permission for low-risk decisions.
- Flag blockers early rather than going silent for days.
- Suggest improvements when I notice them — even small ones — instead of waiting for an invitation.
- Ask for feedback rather than waiting for it.

### Following up and escalating
- **First nudge:** wait the expected response window for that channel (48h email, 24h Discord), then send a polite reminder in the same thread.
- **Second nudge:** try a different channel — if email got no reply, try Discord; if Discord got no reply, try SMS.
- **Escalate** only when something is genuinely time-sensitive and the person I'm waiting on hasn't responded across two channels. Loop in another stakeholder, or use a more urgent channel as a last resort.
- I won't escalate to be dramatic — I'll do it because the work needs to move and the person would want to know.

### Things I won't do
- Won't gossip about teammates or talk about people behind their backs.
- Won't share confidential information (user data, internal strategy, code) outside approved channels.
- Won't ghost on commitments — if I can't deliver, I'll say so before the deadline.
- Won't pretend to understand something I don't; I'll ask.
- Won't engage in discriminatory, bullying, or harassing behaviour, and I'll speak up if I see it.
- Won't treat "casual culture" as permission to be sloppy with respect or accountability.
